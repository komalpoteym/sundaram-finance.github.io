Home Loan Overview

Home Loan refers to a loan availed to purchase a property by offering to pledge the same property as collateral. A home or housing loan may also be referred to as a mortgage. The funds borrowed in the form of a home loan from a bank or NBFC are generally high-value offerings. Such a loan may be repaid in Equated Monthly Instalments (EMI) generally over a long period. Repayment tenures range from 10 years to 20 years. The lender charges a certain percentage of the loan as interest. The title of a property purchased via a home loan transfers back to the borrower after repayment of the loan.

Home loan also refers to an amount borrowed from a bank or an NBFC for the purchase, construction, expansion, or renovation of a house, where the property title remains as security with the lender for the period of the loan.

Home Loans also feature Balance Transfers and Top-up Loans. A Balance Transfer facility gives you the opportunity to transfer your outstanding home loan balance from your current lender, to a new lender who offers you a better rate of interest, and better terms and conditions. A Top-Up Loan likewise allows you access to more funds that have no end-use restrictions over and above your current Home Loan, at attractive rates of interest.
